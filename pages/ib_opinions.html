IB Opinions

Describe IB in One Word

The #1 response was “work”, followed by similar terms such as “pain”, “hard”, and “stressful”. Many of the responses shared a similarity with each other, mainly pertaining to some type of hardship or difficulty. There were some responses that described IB as “motivating”, “interesting”, and “supplemental”, but those kinds of responses were few and far between.

What is your favorite part of IB?

Common Responses: – Friends and classmates – Beta Chi(IB-only social club) – Looks good for colleges – Nice teachers – The sense of community/exclusivity – Nothing is good

Is IB Worth It?

Almost 2/3 of the IB students labeled IB as dependent on the student, while about a quarter did not believe it was worth it, and a minority of 12 percent believed it was worth it. The other 20 % were not in IB.

What is your least favorite part of IB?

Common Responses: – Work – Stress – Essays and IAs – Competitive atmosphere – Overwhelming

Student Interviews

What is One Tip You Would Give to Incoming Freshmen?

Freshman: Make sure to manage your time wisely because there is more work than you are used to doing. Sophomore: My one tip for IB is to have a positive mentality throughout IB because it makes getting through assignments so much easier. Junior: Try to make good friends. Make sure to surround yourself with helpful people that are driven students. Senior: As a freshman, one thing you should keep in mind is preparing for your high school future and beyond. Every senior realizes in their senior year just how quickly time flies and you are left wishing you had done more.

Why Did You Do IB?

Freshman: I did IB because I wanted to try it out and learn better English skills. Sophomore: I joined IB because my sister took it and enjoyed it so I also chose to take it. Junior: I did it because my parents made me and my sister did it before me. Senior: I did IB because my parents made me do it and I wanted to challenge myself academically.
