Dual Enrollment @ Seminole State College

What is Dual Enrollment?

Dual Enrollment is a program students can choose that allows them to take college courses either in-person or online while simultaneously taking their normal high school classes. This allows the student to work towards their high school diploma while also working towards a collegiate degree. The Dual Enrollment Program offered at Seminole State College allows students who qualify to take authentic college classes. Students can earn credit for high school and college simultaneously with the program! This credit can go towards an Associate in Arts (A.A) or an Associate in Science (A.S.) degree. Furthermore, students can work towards a career certificate at the same time. Students can choose two different paths for Dual Enrollment: The General Education path provides a route toward an Associate in Arts (A.A degree). The A.A is guaranteed and reinsured by Seminole State to transfer to any Florida public institution. An unweighted high school GPA of 3.0 is required to qualify for this program. The Career Path provides a route toward an Associate in Science (A.S) degree or a career certificate that can lead Students into an A.S degree in select programs such as Computers, business, Criminal Justice, etc. An unweighted high school GPA of 2.5 is required to qualify for this program. Students will have to take the P.E.R.T exam to see what classes they can be eligible for in the Dual Enrollment program.

How can you apply?

  1. Email or schedule a meeting with your counselor.
  2. Please use this google form link, as this is essential to register for Dual Enrollment at Seminole High School.
  3. Your counselor will inform you about Dual Enrollment, and will help you get into contact with Seminole State to join the Dual Enrollment program.
  4. Prepare yourself for the P.E.R.T exam as this will determine what courses you can take at Seminole State.

Pros & Cons of Dual Enrollment

Pros:

  • Students can earn credit for high school and college simultaneously. One semester of Dual Enrollment classes is equivalent to a full year.
  • This program allows students to shift from being in High-School, to be prepared for College. Dual Enrollment students can physically go to Seminole State College, and participate in extracurriculars!
  • Dual Enrollment is very cost-effective, helping close the college affordability gap. Seminole High covers fees for the classes, including all textbooks! Dual Enrollment is a great way to save money.
  • Credit Transfers to State Schools
  • Graduate Early with an A.A Degree, or save time in College!
  • More likely to continue education. 88% of Dual Enrollment students continued education after high school (Columbia University study)
  • Unlike AP, you only have to pass the class to receive College credit, while AP relies solely on the AP Exam.

Cons:

  • Credits may not Transfer to private colleges
  • Courses can be rigorous and challenging to students
  • Not passing dual enrollment classes may significantly hurt students transcripts, as they are legitimate college courses.
  • May miss crucial high school classes. For example, students taking English as a Dual Enrollment course and not a high school course may hurt.
  • Placed with older students, may have students feeling uncomfortable
  • May impact athletic eligibility.
